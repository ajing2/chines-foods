## Ingredients

**Main**
- 500g pork belly (choose a piece with well-layered fat and lean)

**Seasonings**
- 3 tbsp light soy sauce
- 1 tbsp dark soy sauce
- 2 tbsp Shaoxing rice wine
- 30g rock sugar
- 2 star anise
- 1 small piece of cinnamon
- 4 slices of ginger
- 2 spring onion stalks
- Water as needed

---

## Steps

### 1. Blanch the pork

Cut pork belly into ~3cm cubes. Place in cold water with rice wine and ginger, bring to a boil, skim off the foam, blanch for 3 minutes, then rinse with warm water.

### 2. Caramelize the sugar

Heat a little oil in a wok, add rock sugar over low heat. Stir until it melts and turns amber, then quickly add the pork and toss to coat.

> **Tip**: Keep the heat low. Once the color deepens, add the pork immediately — burnt sugar turns bitter.

### 3. Braise

Add soy sauces, rice wine, star anise, cinnamon, and spring onion. Pour in enough hot water to just cover the pork. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a low simmer with the lid on for 60 minutes.

### 4. Reduce the sauce

Remove the lid and increase heat to reduce the sauce, stirring occasionally, until it thickens and coats the pork in a glossy glaze.

---

## Serving

Garnish with sliced spring onion. Serve over steamed white rice — the sauce over rice is the best part!
